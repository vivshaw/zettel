- # Chapter 3 - Why systems work so well
	- systems work well because of three characteristics: [[resilience]], [[self-organization]], and [[hierarchy]]
	- resilience:
		- references #[[C.S. Holling]] to intro the topic
		- can be thought of as, "the ability to bounce or spring back into shape, position, etc., after being pressed or stretched. Elasticity. The ability to recover strength, spirits, good humor, or any other aspect quickly."
		- a measure of a system’s ability to survive and persist within a variable environment
		- opposites: brittleness, rigidity
		- resilience is created when there are many balancing loops, operating at different scales, over different times, with redundancy. it can even exist at the meta-level, with feedback loops that restore other feedback loops.
		- not the same thing as remaining static through time! resilient systems can be highly dynamic
