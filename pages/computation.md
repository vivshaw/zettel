- A computational procedure is a formalized way of describing **how** to compute a result.
- [[Turing]] was the first to mathematically formalize them. [[Turing machine]]s define what counts as a computational procedure.
	- The [[Church-Turing thesis]] proposes: anything computable by a physical machine is computable by a Turing machine.
