- one or more possible outcomes of a situation or experiment, to which a probability is assigned
- events can be:
	- **mututally exclusive** (conversely **non-exclusive**), when only one may take place
	- **independent** (conversely **dependent**), when the occurrence of one doesn't influence probability of the other
- we can combine and compare events with set notation:
	- $A \cup B$ ([[union (math)]]) means any outcome of either A or B
	- $A \cap B$ ([[intersection]]) is all the outcomes in _both_ A and B
	- $A^c$ ([[complement]]) is the set of all events in the [[sample space]] that aren't in $A$
	- if $A$ and $B$ are [[disjoint]], that is to say they have no events in common, $A \cap B = \varnothing$
- two events are **independent** if $P(A|B) = P(A)$ and $P(B|A) = P(B)$
