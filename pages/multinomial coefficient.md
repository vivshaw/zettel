- a generalization of [[combination]]s into multiple groups. the number of ways to divide $n$ distinct objects into $m$ distinct groups of sizes $k_1, K-2, ...$
- worked example
	- suppose you roll 6d6 and want to know the chances you'll get 3 1's and 3 2's. you know the full set of possibilities, $6^6 = 46656$. then, take 6 choose 3 (20) and 3 choose 3 (1). it's 20/46656.
	- alternately, $\dfrac{6!}{3! * 3!}$
